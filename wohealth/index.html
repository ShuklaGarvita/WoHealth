Why to track your periods??

Keeping track of your period helps you learn more about its frequency and length. It can also help you see patterns in mood changes. Being in tune with your body and understanding the different hormones involved in menstruation can help you navigate your monthly cycle better.

About WoHealth

Different cycle prediction techniques,
and Information regarding preparation for conception, pregnancy, early motherhood, and menopause.
Our mission is to empower women with knowledge about their bodies so that they can make informed decisions about their health and well-being.

Cycle prediction techniques

1. First seven days are of Menstruation
2. Day 8-13 is Proliferative Phase
3. Day 14 is for Ovulation
4. Day 15-28 is Secretory Phase

Preparation for conception

There are a variety of ways you can prepare your body for pregnancy:
1. Avoid alcohol
2. Eat a healthy diet
3. Eliminate exposure to harmful substances
4. Get regular exercise
5. Reduce risk of infections

Early motherhood guidance

First time MOM tips:
1. Trust your gut
2. Allow others to help you
3. Take care of yourself
4. Be ok with pivoting & things not being perfect

Menopause information

Most women experience menopause between the ages of 45 and 55 years as a natural part of biological ageing. Menopause is caused by the loss of ovarian follicular function and a decline in circulating blood oestrogen levels. The menopausal transition can be gradual, usually beginning with changes in the menstrual cycle.
